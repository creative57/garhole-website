What The Gar Hole Actually Is

A century-old bank building in Westminster, Texas. Bonnie and Clyde robbed it. The vault is still there. You turned a biker bar into a guitar culture community — weekly Pickin’ Circles where educators, cowboys, doctors, bikers, and hippies sit in a circle and play whatever they’ve been working on. No wrong notes. No wrong people.

D Magazine called it one of the best roadhouses in North Texas. You call it a frontier Cheers.
Your own tagline: “It’s not really a place. It’s a state of mind.”

None of that is on the website. A GoDaddy template cannot hold what you’ve built.

03 / 07
The Gar Hole is one of the most authentic music venues in North Texas.
Its website is a GoDaddy template with a broken tickets page and a PDF calendar.
That gap is costing you every weekend.

The first thing a visitor reads on garhole.net today is: “PLEASE SELECT ‘VIEW ALL PRODUCTS’ TAB AT BOTTOM OF PAGE TO SEE ALL Featured SHOWS.” There is no tab. The tickets page renders blank. The music calendar is a PDF. The bottom of every page reads: Powered by GoDaddy.

Every person who hits that blank tickets page and leaves is a lost cover charge, a lost bar tab, a lost regular.

04 / 07
The Approach
The website should feel like walking in the door.

Every design decision pulled from the real place. Colors from actual interior photographs. The motion from the real signs on the real walls — bulbs that breathe, neon that flickers because it’s old. The result is a site that people who’ve never been feel compelled to visit. And people who go every Wednesday feel proud to share.

The Anna and Collin County area is growing faster than almost anywhere in Texas. New people arriving every month who have never heard of Westminster. They are looking for exactly what the Gar Hole is. Right now when they search, they find a blank page.

What This Site Does
It tells people who you are

It works for you
Day, band name, save. Done. No GoDaddy dashboard. No re-uploading PDFs. You run a place — the website runs itself.
